# Festival & Showcase Submissions

Find festivals, prepare applications, submit, and track responses.

## When to Activate

- "How do I get booked at festivals?" "Apply to SXSW"
- "Find festivals for my genre" "Festival submission deadlines"
- "I want to play [festival name]"
- Artist's goals mention festival appearances

## Festival Tiers

| Tier | Examples | How to Get In | Typical Pay |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Local/emerging** | Local music fests, block parties | Open applications, free | $0-200 + exposure |
| **Regional showcases** | Indie showcases, college fests | Application + curated | $200-1,000 |
| **Industry conferences** | SXSW, CMJ, A3C, MIDEM | Application ($40-75 fee) + jury | $0-500 (pay-to-play common) |
| **Mid-tier festivals** | Pitchfork, Bonnaroo, Governors Ball | Agent + booking history | $2,000-15,000 |
| **Major festivals** | Coachella, Lollapalooza, Glastonbury | Agent required + major buzz | $10,000-500,000+ |

## Application Requirements

### Standard Requirements (almost every festival asks for)
- [ ] Artist/band name and members
- [ ] Genre and subgenre
- [ ] Bio (150-300 words)
- [ ] 2-3 streaming links (Spotify, Apple Music, SoundCloud)
- [ ] Live performance video (YouTube/Vimeo — **this is the most important**)
- [ ] Press photos (high-res, landscape + portrait)
- [ ] Social media links and follower counts
- [ ] Monthly Spotify listeners
- [ ] Website / EPK link
- [ ] Contact info (email, phone, manager if applicable)
- [ ] Desired set length
- [ ] Technical requirements (basic or full tech rider)
- [ ] Draw estimate ("how many people will come to see you?")

### Often Required
- [ ] Previously played festivals/notable shows
- [ ] Press coverage links
- [ ] Upcoming release info
- [ ] Merch photos (some festivals want to see your brand)
- [ ] Music video links
- [ ] Why this festival? (1-2 sentences — make it specific)

## Application Template

When drafting a festival application:

```
ARTIST: [Name]
GENRE: [Genre/Subgenre]

BIO:
[150-250 word bio — tailored to this festival's vibe. Don't use the
same bio for a hip-hop festival and an indie showcase. Reference
artists on their past lineups if your sound is comparable.]

MUSIC:
• Spotify: [link]
• Apple Music: [link]
• Latest single: "[Title]" — [link]

LIVE VIDEO:
• [YouTube/Vimeo link to best live performance]
  [Context: venue, date, approximate crowd size]

NOTABLE:
• [X] monthly Spotify listeners
• [Previous festival/notable show]
• [Press feature or playlist placement]
• [Any connection to the festival's city/scene]

WHY [FESTIVAL NAME]:
[2-3 sentences that are SPECIFIC to this festival. NOT "we love
your festival." Instead: "We played [city] three times last year
and built a strong local following. Our sound sits between [artist
who played last year] and [another artist who played], and we think
our live energy would be a great fit for [specific stage/time slot]."]

TECH REQUIREMENTS:
• Set length requested: [30/45/60 min]
• Band members on stage: [X]
• Backline needed: [drums, bass amp, guitar amp, keys, or "full backline"]
• Special requirements: [tracks playback, DJ setup, etc.]

CONTACT:
• Booking: [email]
• Management: [email]
• Phone: [number]
```

## Tips That Actually Matter

1. **Live video is everything.** Festivals book you based on your live show, not your streams. A 60-second clip of you commanding a 200-person room beats 100K monthly listeners.
2. **Apply to festivals you can realistically get.** Skip Coachella. Apply to 20 local/regional festivals.
3. **Tailor every application.** Generic apps get deleted. Reference their lineup, their city, their vibe.
4. **Apply early.** Most festivals fill slots on a rolling basis. First week > last day.
5. **Follow up once.** Two weeks after deadline, email: "Just confirming you received my submission for [Festival]. Happy to provide anything else you need." Then stop.
6. **Play the city before the festival.** If a festival is in Austin, play Austin 2-3 times first. Local draw = leverage.
