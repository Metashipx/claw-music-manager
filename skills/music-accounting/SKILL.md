# Music Accounting

Track income, expenses, deductions, and prepare for tax season.

## When to Activate

- "How much am I making?" "Track my expenses," "tax deductions"
- "Am I profitable?" "What can I write off?" "Tax prep"
- End of quarter / end of year financial review
- Artist mentions money, budget, or financial planning

## IMPORTANT: Not a CPA

This skill provides financial education and tracking templates. For actual tax filing, hire a CPA or tax professional who understands entertainment industry taxation. Typical cost: $300-800 for a simple return, $800-2,000 for complex returns.

## Expense Tracking

### Common Music Business Expenses (ALL tax deductible)

| Category | Examples | Deductible? |
|---|---|---|
| **Recording** | Studio time, engineer fees, session musicians, mixing, mastering | Yes — 100% |
| **Equipment** | Instruments, microphones, audio interface, cables, stands | Yes — depreciate or Section 179 |
| **Software** | DAW (Logic, Ableton), plugins, subscriptions | Yes — 100% |
| **Distribution** | DistroKid, TuneCore fees | Yes — 100% |
| **Marketing** | Social media ads, PR services, music videos, photography | Yes — 100% |
| **Travel** | Gas, flights, hotels, rental car, tolls, parking (for music purposes) | Yes — 100% of business portion |
| **Meals** | Meals while traveling for music, client meals | Yes — 50% deductible |
| **Home studio** | Portion of rent/mortgage, utilities, internet (dedicated space) | Yes — based on square footage % |
| **Education** | Music lessons, workshops, courses, conferences | Yes — if related to your music business |
| **Legal** | Attorney fees, contract review, copyright registration | Yes — 100% |
| **Insurance** | Equipment insurance, liability insurance | Yes — 100% |
| **Merch inventory** | Cost of goods (shirts, vinyl, stickers) | Yes — COGS |
| **Subscriptions** | Spotify for Artists, Canva, email marketing, website hosting | Yes — 100% |
| **Phone** | Business use portion of your phone bill | Yes — business % |
| **Commission** | Manager %, agent %, producer points | Yes — 100% |

## Tax Season Prep

### Quarterly Estimated Taxes

If you expect to owe >$1,000 in taxes, the IRS requires quarterly estimated payments:
- **Q1**: April 15
- **Q2**: June 15
- **Q3**: September 15
- **Q4**: January 15 (of next year)

**Estimate**: Set aside 25-30% of net profit each month in a separate savings account.

### Year-End Checklist

- [ ] Compile all 1099s (1099-MISC from venues, 1099-K from Shopify/DistroKid/etc.)
- [ ] Total all income by category
- [ ] Total all expenses by category
- [ ] Calculate home studio deduction (square footage of studio ÷ total home square footage × rent/mortgage)
- [ ] List all equipment purchases >$2,500 (for depreciation or Section 179)
- [ ] Calculate mileage deduction ($0.67/mile for 2024, keep a log)
- [ ] Gather receipts for all deductions
- [ ] Review health insurance (self-employed artists can deduct 100% of premiums)
- [ ] Check retirement contributions (SEP-IRA: up to 25% of net self-employment income)

### Business Structure

| Structure | Best For | Self-Employment Tax? |
|---|---|---|
| **Sole proprietor** (default) | Earning <$50K/year | Yes — 15.3% on all profit |
| **LLC** | Earning $50K+, want liability protection | Yes — unless elect S-Corp |
| **S-Corp** | Earning $80K+, want to reduce self-employment tax | Only on "reasonable salary," not distributions |

**When to form an LLC**: When music income consistently exceeds $50K/year, or you want liability protection (e.g., someone gets hurt at your show).

**When to elect S-Corp**: When net profit exceeds ~$80K/year. Pay yourself a "reasonable salary" (say $40K), take the rest as distributions (no self-employment tax on distributions). Saves $5,000-15,000+/year in taxes.

## Financial Red Flags

Alert the artist if:
- Expenses exceed income for 3+ consecutive months (unless investing in a release)
- No tax savings set aside (should be 25-30% of net income)
- Equipment purchases not tracked (missing deductions)
- No mileage log kept (losing $0.67/mile deduction)
- Merch inventory cost exceeds merch revenue
- Streaming revenue declining while marketing spend is increasing
