# Music Lawyer

Legal guidance for independent artists. Know enough to protect yourself and know when to hire a professional.

## When to Activate

- "Should I sign this?" "Is this deal good?" "Contract review"
- "Copyright," "trademark," "splits," "publishing deal"
- "Do I need a lawyer?" "What are my rights?"
- Any document or deal terms shared for review

## CRITICAL DISCLAIMER

**This skill provides legal education and red flag detection, NOT legal advice.** The agent is not a lawyer. For any contract worth more than $1,000, any publishing deal, any label deal, or any legal dispute — **hire a real entertainment attorney.**

## Contract Red Flags

When the artist shares a contract, scan for these immediately:

### CRITICAL RED FLAGS (walk away or get a lawyer)

| Red Flag | Why It's Dangerous |
|---|---|
| **"In perpetuity"** | They own your work forever. Should be a defined term (3-7 years typical) |
| **360 deal with no advance** | They take a cut of everything (music, merch, touring, endorsements) but invest nothing upfront |
| **"All rights assigned"** | You're giving away ownership. Should be "licensed" not "assigned" unless selling outright |
| **No reversion clause** | If the label goes bankrupt or stops promoting, you can't get your masters back |
| **Cross-collateralization** | Losses from one album offset profits from another. Your hit pays for their flop |
| **Controlled composition clause** | Caps your mechanical royalty rate at 75% of statutory. Costs you money on every stream |
| **Option periods without limits** | Label can keep extending the deal without new terms. You're locked in indefinitely |
| **No audit rights** | You can't verify their accounting. Non-negotiable — always require audit rights |
| **Non-compete broader than music** | Prevents you from creating ANY content, not just competing music |
| **Assignment without consent** | They can sell your contract to anyone without asking you |

### YELLOW FLAGS (negotiate, don't walk)

| Flag | What to Negotiate |
|---|---|
| Advance is recoupable from all sources | Limit recoupment to recording costs only |
| 50/50 net profit split | Push for 60/40 or 70/30 in your favor, especially if self-funded |
| Territory: "worldwide" | Consider if you need worldwide or just specific territories |
| First right of refusal on next project | Limit to 30-60 days, not open-ended |
| Approval rights are "reasonable" | Define what "reasonable" means in writing |

## Common Deal Structures

### Distribution Deal (Best for Indies)
```
What: Distributor places your music on platforms
You keep: 80-100% of royalties (minus distributor fee)
They get: 0-20% distribution fee
Term: 1-3 years, non-exclusive preferred
Masters: YOU OWN THEM
Best for: Artists who fund their own recording
Examples: DistroKid (keep 100%), TuneCore (keep 100%), CD Baby (keep 91%)
```

### License Deal (Good Middle Ground)
```
What: Label licenses your finished masters for a term
You keep: 50-80% of royalties
They get: 20-50% for marketing/distribution
Term: 3-7 years, then rights revert to you
Masters: YOU OWN THEM (licensed, not assigned)
Advance: $5K-100K typical for indie labels
Best for: Artists with finished product who need marketing push
```

### Traditional Label Deal (Proceed with Caution)
```
What: Label funds recording, owns masters
You keep: 12-20% of revenue (after recoupment)
They get: 80-88% of revenue
Term: Multiple albums, option-heavy
Masters: LABEL OWNS THEM (possibly forever)
Advance: $50K-500K+ (recoupable)
Best for: Artists who need significant funding and are OK giving up ownership
WARNING: Most artists never recoup. You earn $0 until the advance is paid back.
```

### Publishing Deal
```
What: Publisher manages your songwriting copyrights
Types:
- Full publishing: Publisher gets 50% of publishing (you keep writer's share)
- Co-publishing: Publisher gets 25% of total (you keep 75%)
- Admin deal: Publisher gets 10-20% commission (you keep 80-90%)

Best for indies: Admin deal (Songtrust, CD Baby Pro)
Only take full/co-pub if: significant advance offered ($25K+)
```

## Copyright Basics

### What's Automatically Copyrighted
The moment you record a song, you own two copyrights:
1. **Sound recording copyright (℗)** — the specific recording (master)
2. **Musical composition copyright (©)** — the song itself (lyrics + melody)

You don't need to register to own copyright. But registration gives you:
- Ability to sue for infringement in US courts
- Statutory damages ($750-$150,000 per infringement)
- Attorney's fees recovery

### How to Register Copyright
1. Go to https://www.copyright.gov/registration/
2. File as "Sound Recording" (covers both recording and composition)
3. Fee: $65 per registration (single song), $65 for a group of up to 10 songs by same author
4. Processing: 3-6 months
5. Effective date: the date you filed, not the date they process it

### Register BEFORE Release
If you register within 3 months of publication, you can claim statutory damages and attorney fees. After 3 months, you can only claim actual damages (much harder to prove).

## Splits Agreements

**ALWAYS document splits BEFORE releasing a song.** More careers are damaged by split disputes than bad deals.

### Split Sheet Template

```
SPLIT SHEET

Song Title: _____________________
Recording Date: _________________
Release Date: ___________________

WRITERS:
Name: ________________________
PRO: ASCAP / BMI
IPI #: _______________________
Ownership %: _________________
Role: Lyrics / Music / Both

Name: ________________________
PRO: ASCAP / BMI
IPI #: _______________________
Ownership %: _________________
Role: Lyrics / Music / Both

MASTER RECORDING OWNERS:
Name: ________________________
Ownership %: _________________

PRODUCER:
Name: ________________________
Points: ______ (typical: 2-5 points = 2-5% of master revenue)
Advance: $_______ (recoupable? Y/N)

Total writer shares: 100%
Total master shares: 100%

Signatures:
_________________________ Date: _______
_________________________ Date: _______
```

### Standard Split Guidelines

| Contributor | Typical Share |
|---|---|
| Sole writer (lyrics + music) | 100% of publishing |
| Two co-writers (equal) | 50/50 |
| Writer + producer who contributed melody | Negotiate (common: 70/30 or 60/40) |
| Beat maker (bought exclusive beat) | 0-50% of publishing (depends on contract) |
| Topliner (melody + lyrics over a beat) | 50% of publishing (typical) |
| Sample owner | Negotiated — can be 25-100% of publishing |

## Trademark

### When to Trademark Your Artist Name
- After you've been using it consistently for 1+ year
- Before you have significant revenue/following
- Cost: $250-350 per class (file in Class 41 for entertainment services)
- File at: https://www.uspto.gov/trademarks

### Before Filing, Search
1. USPTO TESS database: https://tess2.uspto.gov
2. State trademark databases
3. Social media handle availability
4. Domain availability
5. Existing artists on Spotify/Apple Music with the same name

## When to Hire a Real Lawyer

**Immediately hire an entertainment attorney if:**
- Label or publisher offers a deal with an advance over $5,000
- You're signing anything longer than 2 pages
- Someone claims you infringed their copyright
- You need to send a cease & desist
- You're forming an LLC or business entity for your music
- Revenue exceeds $50K/year (tax + entity structuring)
- You're licensing music for a major sync (TV show, film, national ad)
- A co-writer or producer disputes their split
- Anyone says "you don't need a lawyer for this"

**Questions to ask a music lawyer before hiring:**
1. Do you specialize in music/entertainment law?
2. What's your fee structure? (hourly, flat, percentage)
3. Can you provide references from independent artists?
4. Do you represent labels too? (potential conflict of interest)
5. Will you explain the contract to me, not just review it?

## Key Legal Terms Glossary

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Advance** | Upfront payment, usually recoupable from future royalties |
| **Recoupable** | The label recoups (takes back) the advance from your earnings before you see royalties |
| **Mechanical royalty** | Paid per reproduction of a song (stream, download, physical copy) |
| **Performance royalty** | Paid when a song is performed publicly (radio, live, streaming) |
| **Sync fee** | One-time payment for using music in visual media |
| **Master** | The specific sound recording |
| **Publishing** | The underlying composition (lyrics + melody) |
| **Reversion** | Rights return to the artist after a defined period |
| **Work for hire** | You created it, but the hiring party owns it (avoid for your own music) |
| **Compulsory license** | Legal right to cover any released song by paying statutory rate |
| **Statutory rate** | $0.12 per copy for songs under 5 minutes (2024) |
| **Points** | Percentage points of revenue (1 point = 1%) |
| **All-in** | Producer points come out of artist's share, not on top |
