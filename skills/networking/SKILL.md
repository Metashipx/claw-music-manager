# Networking — Build Real Industry Relationships

Genuine connections, not transactional follower swaps.

## When to Activate

- "How do I network?" "I don't know anyone in the industry"
- "I'm going to [conference/event] — how do I make the most of it?"
- "How do I connect with [producers/A&Rs/bookers/artists]?"
- "I need to build my team"
- Artist is isolated, not connected to their local scene

## The Networking Mindset

**Rule #1: Lead with value, not asks.**

Every interaction should leave the other person thinking "that was worth my time." Not "that person wants something from me."

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| "Check out my track" to a stranger | Comment genuinely on their work for 3 weeks, then share yours naturally |
| "Can you put me on?" | "I loved your show at [venue]. If you ever need an opener, I'd be honored." |
| "Follow for follow?" | Actually follow, engage, be a real fan |
| Hand out 50 business cards at a conference | Have 5 real conversations |

### How to Research
1. **Instagram location tags** — search venue names, see who performs there
2. **Spotify "Fans Also Like"** — find artists in your lane in your city
3. **Local music publications** — who are they writing about?
4. **Event listings** — who's promoting shows in your genre?
5. **Open mics and showcases** — show up, observe, talk to people AFTER

## Conference & Event Strategy

### Before the Event
1. **Research the attendees/speakers/performers.** Make a hit list of 5-10 people to meet.
2. **Study their work.** Know their recent projects so you can reference them genuinely.
3. **Prepare your intro.** 15 seconds: name, what you do, one specific thing. Not a pitch — a conversation starter.
4. **Bring cards or a QR code** that links to your Spotify/smart link. NOT a demo USB.
5. **Set realistic goals.** 3 genuine connections > 30 surface-level handshakes.

### At the Event
- **Arrive early.** Fewer people = easier to start conversations.
- **Don't hover around famous people.** Talk to the people around you. Everyone at industry events matters.
- **Ask questions, don't pitch.** "What are you working on?" > "Listen to my track."
- **Take notes immediately after.** Name, what you talked about, follow-up item. Your phone notes app is fine.
- **Go to the after-parties.** That's where real relationships form.
- **Eat with people.** Meals create bonds that panels don't.

### After the Event
- **Follow up within 48 hours.** DM or email: "Great meeting you at [event]. I really liked what you said about [specific thing]. Let's stay connected."
- **Connect them to someone.** "I met someone at [event] who does [thing you're looking for]. Want me to introduce you?" This is the highest-value move.
- **Don't ask for anything in the first follow-up.** Just establish the connection.

## Building Your Team

When the artist is ready to add people:

### Who You Need (In Order)

1. **A great engineer** (first hire — better mixes = better everything)
2. **A photographer** (press photos, content, show documentation)
3. **A graphic designer** (cover art, visual identity, merch design)
4. **A booking agent** (when you're consistently drawing 100+ in multiple markets)
5. **A publicist** (when you have something worth publicizing — not before)
6. **A manager** (when the business side takes more than 10 hours/week of your time)
7. **A lawyer** (when deals start happening — not before)

### How to Find Good People
- **Go to shows.** The engineer who mixes the best live sound in your city is probably available for studio work.
- **Ask other artists.** "Who shot your press photos?" is a better question than any Google search.
- **Check credits.** Who mixed/mastered/produced the local releases you admire?
- **Start with trades.** Before you can afford to pay, offer what you have — a feature, a shoutout, cross-promotion.

## Online Networking

### The 3-Month Relationship Build

For someone you want to connect with (a producer, A&R, booker, artist):

**Month 1:** Follow, engage genuinely. Like and comment on their posts. Not "fire emoji" — actual thoughts. "The chord progression at 1:45 in this is insane" shows you listened.

**Month 2:** Share their work. Tag them. "Been playing this on repeat" with a story repost. DM something specific: "Your set at [venue] was incredible — how did you get that bass sound?"

**Month 3:** Now you can make the ask. "I've been working on something that's in a similar lane — would love to get your thoughts when you have a minute." By now they know your name and face.

**This sounds slow. It works.**
