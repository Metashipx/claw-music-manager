# Playlist Pitcher

Find curators, craft pitches, and track playlist placements.

## When to Activate

- "Pitch my song to playlists," "find playlists for my genre"
- "Did any playlists add my track?" "Playlist strategy"
- New release approaching — proactive playlist outreach

## Playlist Types

| Type | How to Get On | Timeline | Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Spotify Editorial** | Spotify for Artists pitch tool | Submit 4 weeks pre-release | Highest — 10K-1M+ streams |
| **Algorithmic** (Discover Weekly, Release Radar) | Good save rate + engagement | Automatic based on listener behavior | High — consistent streams |
| **Independent Curators** | Direct outreach | 1-2 weeks response time | Medium — 100-50K followers |
| **Apple Music Editorial** | Relationship-based, no portal | Ongoing relationship building | High — Apple pays more per stream |

## Spotify Editorial Pitch

Submit via Spotify for Artists (artists.spotify.com):

**Required info:**
- Song description (what it's about, the story behind it)
- Genre and subgenre
- Mood (energetic, chill, melancholy, etc.)
- Instruments featured
- Cultural context (if relevant)
- Similar artists (2-3 names listeners would recognize)

**Tips:**
- Submit on Tuesday (editorial team reviews Tue-Thu)
- Submit exactly 4 weeks before release (not earlier, not later)
- Be specific: "recorded in a cabin in Vermont during a breakup" > "this song is about love"
- Mention if there's a music video, press coverage, or notable collaborator

## Independent Curator Outreach

### Finding Curators

Search for playlists matching the track's genre/mood:
1. Spotify search: genre + mood keywords (e.g., "late night R&B," "indie road trip")
2. SubmitHub (submithub.com) — paid submissions to verified curators
3. PlaylistPush (playlistpush.com) — automated matching service
4. Social media: search #playlistcurator on Instagram/Twitter
5. Daily Playlists (dailyplaylists.com) — free submissions

### Pitch Template

```
Subject: [Genre] submission — "[Track Title]" by [Artist]

Hey [Curator Name],

I've been listening to [Playlist Name] — [specific detail about the playlist
that shows you actually listened to it, e.g., "the transition from SZA into
Frank Ocean was perfect"].

I just released "[Track Title]" and think it could fit the vibe. It's a
[genre] track about [one sentence about the song]. Sonically, it sits
somewhere between [Artist A] and [Artist B].

Spotify link: [link]

No pressure either way — just thought it'd be a good fit.

[Your Name]
```

### Pitch Rules

1. **Personalize every pitch.** Mention the specific playlist and why your track fits.
2. **Keep it under 100 words.** Curators get hundreds of submissions.
3. **Never ask for payment.** Payola is against Spotify TOS and can get you banned.
4. **Don't pitch to curators outside your genre.** Instant delete.
5. **Follow up once after 1 week.** If no response, move on.
6. **Track responses** in a spreadsheet or notes file.

## Tracking Placements

After pitching, monitor:
- Spotify for Artists → check "Playlists" tab for adds
- Stream count spikes (sudden jump = likely playlist add)
- Save rate (high save rate from playlist listeners = strong signal)

Log all placements:
```
Playlist: [Name]
Followers: [count]
Added: [date]
Removed: [date or "still on"]
Estimated streams from playlist: [count]
```

## Anti-Patterns

- **Botted playlists**: If a playlist has 50K followers but tracks get <100 streams, it's fake. Avoid.
- **Pay-for-play**: Violates Spotify TOS. Can result in track removal or artist ban.
- **Mass email blasts**: Generic pitches get ignored. Always personalize.
- **SubmitHub tip**: Premium submissions ($2-4) get faster reviews, not better reviews. Standard is fine for most artists.
