# Press Kit Generator

Create professional press materials for media outreach.

## When to Activate

- "Write my bio," "I need a press kit," "draft a press release"
- "I'm reaching out to blogs," "need a one-sheet"
- New release needs media materials

## Artist Bio (3 versions)

Always maintain three bio lengths:

### Short Bio (50 words)
For social profiles, playlist descriptions, festival applications.
```
[Artist] is a [genre] artist from [city] known for [signature sound/style].
With [notable achievement — streams, placements, shows], [they/he/she]
blends [influence A] with [influence B] to create [what makes them unique].
[Latest release] is out now.
```

### Medium Bio (150 words)
For press kits, booking inquiries, blog features.
```
[Expand on the story — origin, journey, sound evolution, key moments]
[Notable achievements with specifics — numbers, names, venues]
[What drives the music — themes, mission, artistic vision]
[What's next — upcoming release, tour, project]
```

### Long Bio (300-500 words)
For feature articles, full EPK, grant applications.
```
[Full narrative arc — where they came from, how they got here]
[Musical influences and how they shaped the sound]
[Career milestones in chronological order]
[Critical reception — pull quotes from reviews]
[Personal dimension — what makes them human, relatable]
[Current chapter — what they're working on and where they're headed]
```

## Electronic Press Kit (EPK)

A complete EPK contains:

```
EPK — [Artist Name]
===================

## Bio
[Medium bio]

## Music
- Latest single: "[Title]" — [Spotify link] | [Apple Music link]
- Latest EP/Album: "[Title]" — [link]
- Music video: [YouTube link]

## Press Photos
- High-res landscape: [link to download]
- High-res portrait: [link to download]
- Live performance: [link to download]
(Minimum 2400px wide, 300 DPI preferred, credit: [photographer])

## Notable Achievements
- [X] total streams across platforms
- Featured on [playlist/blog/press]
- Opened for [artist] at [venue]
- Sync placement in [show/film/ad]

## Press Coverage
- "[Quote from review]" — [Publication]
- "[Quote from interview]" — [Publication]

## Social Media
- Instagram: @[handle] ([followers] followers)
- TikTok: @[handle] ([followers] followers)
- Spotify: [monthly listeners] monthly listeners

## Contact
- Booking: [email]
- Management: [email]
- Press: [email]
- General: [email]

## Links
- Website: [url]
- Linktree/linkfire: [url]
- YouTube: [url]
```

## Press Release Template

```
FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

[Artist Name] Releases New [Single/EP/Album] "[Title]"
[One-line hook that makes a journalist want to read more]

[City, State] — [Date] — [Artist Name] releases "[Title]," a
[genre] [single/EP/album] that [what makes it special — produced by
someone notable? Recorded in an interesting way? Features a collaboration?].

[Quote from the artist about the release — 1-2 sentences, personal and
genuine. Not generic "I'm so excited" language.]

[Paragraph about the sound — describe what the listener will hear. Use
specific musical references, not abstract language. "A pitched-down vocal
sample over boom-bap drums" not "a sonic journey."]

[Context paragraph — where this fits in the artist's career. Previous
releases, growth, upcoming plans.]

"[Title]" is available now on all streaming platforms.

Stream "[Title]": [Smart link]
Press photos: [Download link]
EPK: [Link]

###

Press contact: [Name] — [Email]
```

## Writing Rules

1. **Never use**: "sonic journey," "genre-defying," "unique blend," "breaking boundaries"
2. **Always use**: specific references, numbers, names, concrete details
3. **Tone**: professional but not stiff. The artist is a real person, not a product.
4. **Length**: press releases should be 300-400 words max. Journalists skim.
5. **Subject line**: "[Artist] — New [Single/EP] '[Title]' (out [date]) + EPK"
