# PRO & Royalty Agency Registration

Guide artists through registering with every organization that collects money on their behalf.

## When to Activate

- "How do I sign up for ASCAP?" "What's a PRO?" "Am I missing royalties?"
- "Publishing admin," "mechanical royalties," "SoundExchange"
- New artist who hasn't registered anywhere yet
- Artist audit — "am I collecting everything I'm owed?"

## The Royalty Map

Most independent artists leave 30-50% of their royalties uncollected because they don't know these organizations exist.

## Step-by-Step Registration Guide

### 1. Performing Rights Organization (PRO) — MANDATORY

Collects **performance royalties** when your music is played on radio, TV, live venues, streaming, and public spaces.

**Choose ONE:**

| PRO | Fee | Best For | Apply |
|---|---|---|---|
| **ASCAP** | $50 one-time | Most popular, no contract term | https://www.ascap.com/membership/join |
| **BMI** | Free | No fee, slightly slower payments | https://www.bmi.com/join |
| **SESAC** | Invite-only | Smaller, more personal service | https://www.sesac.com |

**ASCAP Registration Walkthrough:**
1. Go to https://www.ascap.com/membership/join
2. Select "Writer" (you write songs) and/or "Publisher" (you self-publish)
3. Fill in: legal name, artist/pen name, date of birth, SSN/TIN, address
4. Pay $50 one-time fee
5. You'll get an **IPI number** (International Party Information) — save this, it's your global ID
6. Register each song in the ASCAP repertory after joining

**BMI Registration Walkthrough:**
1. Go to https://www.bmi.com/join
2. Select "Songwriter" and/or "Publisher"
3. Fill in: legal name, performing name, SSN, address
4. Free to join
5. You'll get an IPI number
6. Register works at https://www.bmi.com/registration

**Important:** You can only be a member of ONE PRO at a time. You cannot switch mid-contract (ASCAP has no contract term, BMI has 2-year terms). Choose carefully.

**Self-publishing:** Register as BOTH a writer AND a publisher. Your publishing entity name must be different from your artist name (e.g., "After Hours Publishing" for artist "Jordan"). This lets you collect both the writer's share AND the publisher's share.

### 2. SoundExchange — MANDATORY

Collects **digital performance royalties** from non-interactive streaming (Pandora, SiriusXM, internet radio, cable music channels). This is separate from what ASCAP/BMI collects.

**Registration:**
1. Go to https://www.soundexchange.com/artist-registration
2. Register as a "Featured Artist" AND as a "Sound Recording Copyright Owner" (if you own your masters)
3. Fill in: legal name, artist name, SSN/TIN
4. Free to join
5. Link your catalog by providing ISRCs

**Most indie artists skip this and lose thousands in uncollected royalties.** SoundExchange holds your money for 3 years, then it goes to the general pool.

### 3. The Mechanical Licensing Collective (MLC) — MANDATORY

Collects **mechanical royalties** from streaming services (Spotify, Apple Music, etc.) in the US. Required by law since 2021.

**Registration:**
1. Go to https://www.themlc.com
2. Create an account as a songwriter/publisher
3. Register your works with ISWCs or match to existing catalog
4. Free to join
5. This is specifically for US mechanical royalties from streaming

### 4. Publishing Administrator — RECOMMENDED

A publishing admin collects royalties you'd otherwise miss — especially international royalties, micro-sync fees, and YouTube Content ID revenue.

| Service | Fee | What They Do |
|---|---|---|
| **Songtrust** | $100 setup + 15% commission | Registers your songs with 60+ collection societies worldwide |
| **CD Baby Pro** | $20/single, $60/album | Publishing admin built into your distributor |
| **TuneCore Publishing** | 15% commission | Same — built into distributor |
| **Sentric Music** | Free tier available | UK-based, good international coverage |

**Songtrust Registration:**
1. Go to https://www.songtrust.com
2. Create account, pay $100 setup
3. Add your songs with metadata (writers, splits, ISRCs)
4. Songtrust registers with collection societies in 100+ countries
5. They collect and pass through royalties minus 15% commission

**When to use a pub admin vs DIY:**
- <10 songs, US-only audience → DIY (register with ASCAP + MLC directly)
- 10+ songs OR international audience → use Songtrust or similar
- Signed publishing deal → your publisher handles this

### 5. Harry Fox Agency (HFA) — SITUATIONAL

Issues **mechanical licenses** for cover songs. If you're covering someone else's song:

1. Go to https://www.harryfox.com
2. Search for the song
3. Request a mechanical license
4. Pay per-unit fee (currently $0.12/song for physical, streaming handled by MLC)

Only needed for covers. Not needed for original music.

### 6. International Collection Societies — IF INTERNATIONAL AUDIENCE

If you have listeners outside the US, your PRO collects internationally through reciprocal agreements, but a publishing admin (Songtrust) is more thorough.

Key international societies:
- **PRS for Music** (UK) — https://www.prsformusic.com
- **SOCAN** (Canada) — https://www.socan.com
- **GEMA** (Germany) — https://www.gema.de
- **SACEM** (France) — https://www.sacem.fr
- **JASRAC** (Japan) — https://www.jasrac.or.jp

You generally don't register directly with these — your US PRO or publishing admin handles it.

## The Registration Checklist

For a new artist, in this order:

- [ ] **ASCAP or BMI** — Choose one, register as writer + publisher ($50 or free)
- [ ] **SoundExchange** — Register as artist + sound recording owner (free)
- [ ] **The MLC** — Register for streaming mechanical royalties (free)
- [ ] **Songtrust or CD Baby Pro** — Publishing admin for worldwide collection ($100 or per-release)
- [ ] **Register all songs** — Add every released track to your PRO and MLC with correct metadata
- [ ] **Save your IPI number** — You'll need it for every registration going forward

## Revenue Impact

For an artist earning $1,000/month from streaming:

| Without registrations | With all registrations |
|---|---|
| ~$600 collected (streaming royalties only) | ~$950-1,000 collected |
| Missing: performance royalties, SoundExchange, international | All royalty streams captured |
| **Leaving ~$400/month on the table** | **Full collection** |

## Common Mistakes

1. **Not registering as a publisher** — You lose 50% of your performance royalties (the publisher's share goes uncollected)
2. **Skipping SoundExchange** — Pandora, SiriusXM, and internet radio royalties go to a pool after 3 years
3. **Not registering songs** — Joining a PRO is not enough. You must register EACH song individually.
4. **Wrong metadata** — Misspelled names, wrong splits, missing co-writers = lost royalties
5. **Registering with multiple PROs** — You can only be with one. Switching mid-contract causes problems.
