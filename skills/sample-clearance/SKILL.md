# Sample Clearance

Research and manage sample clearance for music production.

## When to Activate

- "Can I use this sample?" "How do I clear this?" "Who owns this?"
- Artist mentions sampling, interpolation, or flipping a track
- Any legal question about using someone else's music

## Sample vs Interpolation

| Type | Definition | What to clear |
|---|---|---|
| **Sample** | Using the actual recording | Master rights + Publishing rights |
| **Interpolation** | Re-recording the melody/lyrics | Publishing rights only |
| **Cover** | Performing someone else's song fully | Mechanical license (compulsory) |

## Research Process

When the artist wants to use a sample:

### Step 1: Identify the Original
- Track title, artist, album, year
- Writers/composers (check ASCAP, BMI, SESAC databases)
- Producer(s)
- Label that released it

### Step 2: Find Rights Holders
- **Master rights** (the recording): Usually the record label. Check Discogs, AllMusic, or the label's catalog.
- **Publishing rights** (the composition): Check PRO databases:
  - ASCAP: https://www.ascap.com/repertory
  - BMI: https://repertoire.bmi.com
  - SESAC: https://www.sesac.com
  - PRS (UK): https://www.prsformusic.com
- **If multiple writers**: Each writer's publisher must approve

### Step 3: Assess Clearability
- Major label sample = expensive ($2K-$50K+) and slow (3-6 months)
- Indie label sample = cheaper ($500-$5K) and faster (2-8 weeks)
- Unsigned/independent artist = often free or cheap, fastest
- Dead artist with estate = unpredictable, can take months

### Step 4: Draft Clearance Request

```
Subject: Sample Clearance Request — [Your Song] by [Your Artist Name]

Dear [Rights Holder / Publishing Admin],

I am writing to request clearance to use a sample from "[Original Song Title]"
by [Original Artist] in a new recording titled "[Your Song Title]" by [Your Artist Name].

Sample details:
- Original track: "[Title]" by [Artist]
- Portion used: [describe — e.g., "4-bar drum loop from 0:32-0:40"]
- Duration in new work: [how long the sample plays in your track]
- How it's used: [e.g., "looped as the main beat throughout the track"]

New recording details:
- Title: [Your Song Title]
- Artist: [Your Name]
- Planned release date: [Date]
- Distribution: [DistroKid/TuneCore — digital only / physical + digital]
- Expected reach: [Be honest — "independent release, no major label backing"]

I would appreciate discussing clearance terms including any upfront fee
and/or royalty split arrangement.

Thank you for your time.

[Your Name]
[Contact Info]
```

## Cost Expectations

| Original Track Profile | Typical Cost | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Unknown/indie track | Free - $2,000 | 1-4 weeks |
| Popular indie track | $1,000 - $5,000 | 2-8 weeks |
| Major label catalog | $5,000 - $50,000+ | 2-6 months |
| Classic/iconic sample | $10,000 - $100,000+ | 3-12 months |
| Public domain | Free | Immediate |

## Alternatives If Unclearable

1. **Interpolate instead** — re-record the part, only need publishing clearance
2. **Use royalty-free samples** — Splice, Tracklib (pre-cleared), Loopmasters
3. **Chop it unrecognizably** — if nobody can identify it, it's effectively original (legally gray — not advice, just reality)
4. **Commission a sound-alike** — hire a musician to create something inspired by but legally distinct from the original
5. **Tracklib** — marketplace for pre-cleared samples with transparent pricing

## Warning Signs

- Never release an uncleared sample. Streaming platforms will take it down and you lose all streams.
- "Fair use" almost never applies to music sampling. Don't rely on it.
- If the clearance fee exceeds the expected revenue from the track, reconsider.
- Always get clearance in writing before releasing.
