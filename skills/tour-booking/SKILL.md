# Tour & Show Booking

Find venues, book shows, route tours, and manage the live performance business.

## When to Activate

- "Book me a show," "find venues in [city]," "I want to tour"
- "What venues should I play?" "How do I get booked?"
- "Advance the show," "what's my tour calendar?"
- Artist mentions live performance, gigs, or touring

## Show Tiers — Know Where You Are

| Tier | Venue Capacity | How to Get Booked | Typical Pay |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Open mics / showcases** | 50-100 | Sign up, show up | $0 (exposure) |
| **Local support** | 100-300 | Email talent buyer, bring 20+ people | $50-200 + door split |
| **Headline local** | 100-300 | Proven draw (50+ tickets) | $200-500 + door |
| **Regional support** | 300-800 | Agent or strong pitch, opening for touring act | $300-1,000 |
| **Headline regional** | 300-800 | 100+ ticket guarantee | $500-2,000 + merch |
| **National support** | 800-2,000 | Booking agent required | $500-1,500/night |
| **Headline national** | 500-2,000+ | Agent + strong ticket history | $2,000-10,000/night |
| **Festivals** | 1,000-50,000+ | Agent, press, streaming numbers | $1,000-50,000+ |

## Venue Research

When the artist asks to find venues in a city:

### Research Checklist
1. Search "[city] live music venues [genre]" — compile list
2. For each venue, find:
   - Capacity
   - Talent buyer name and email (check venue website → "booking" or "contact")
   - What kind of acts they book (genre, tier)
   - Typical night (weeknight vs weekend)
   - Backline provided (drums, amps, PA)
   - Age restriction (all ages vs 21+)
   - Door deal vs guarantee vs hybrid

### Key Venue Databases
- **IndieOnTheMove** (indieonthemove.com) — venue database with capacity, contacts, reviews
- **Songkick** (songkick.com) — find similar artists' tour histories to discover venues
- **Bandsintown** (bandsintown.com) — venue discovery, fan demand by city

## Tour Routing

When planning a multi-date run:

### Routing Rules
1. **Max 4-hour drives between shows** (longer = exhausted, bad performance)
2. **Day off every 4-5 shows** (burnout is real)
3. **Anchor dates first** — book the biggest/best shows first, fill in around them
4. **Avoid double-backs** — route should flow geographically (don't play NYC Monday, Boston Tuesday, NYC Wednesday)
5. **Weekends for bigger markets** — save Friday/Saturday for cities where you draw best
6. **Build in load-in time** — most venues want you there 2-4 hours before doors

## Show Advancing

2 weeks before each show, confirm everything:

### Advance Checklist
- [ ] Confirm date, time, and billing (headliner/support/opener)
- [ ] Load-in time
- [ ] Soundcheck time
- [ ] Set length
- [ ] Stage plot sent to venue (band positions, input list)
- [ ] Backline: what's provided, what to bring
- [ ] Hospitality: drink tickets, food, green room
- [ ] Payment: guarantee amount, when/how paid (cash at settlement, check, direct deposit)
- [ ] Merch: where to set up, venue cut (0-25% is typical)
- [ ] Guest list: how many, how to submit names
- [ ] Parking: where to load in, where to park during show
- [ ] Promotion: is venue promoting? Poster provided? Social media?
- [ ] Recording/photography: any restrictions?
- [ ] Curfew: when does music stop?

## Settlement

```
Merch revenue:       $______
Merch venue cut:     $______ ([X]%)
Drink tickets:       [X]

Total paid:          $______
Payment method:      Cash / Check / Venmo / Wire
```

## Key Metrics

Track after every show:
- **Draw**: How many people came specifically for you?
- **Tickets sold**: Your name on the ticket (vs walk-ups for headliner)
- **Merch per head**: Merch revenue ÷ attendance (good = $3-8/person)
- **Cost per show**: Total expenses ÷ total shows
- **Net per show**: Income minus expenses per show

## Growth Strategy

```
Year 1: Local → Regional
  - Play your city 1-2x/month
  - Build to 50+ consistent draw
  - Weekend runs to nearby cities (2-3 hour radius)

Year 2: Regional → National
  - Support slots for touring acts
  - 2-week regional tours
  - Festival applications
  - Consider a booking agent (agents take 10-15%)

Year 3: National → Sustainable
  - Headline 300-500 cap venues
  - Full national tours (3-4 weeks)
  - Festival circuit
  - Tour support for bigger acts
```
