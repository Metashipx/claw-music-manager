# Visual Identity — Look Like You Sound

Build a cohesive visual brand that matches the music.

## When to Activate

- "I need a logo" "My branding sucks" "How should my covers look?"
- "I want to look more professional" "Visual identity"
- Intake reveals missing visual assets (no logo, no brand colors, no press photos)
- New project/era that needs a visual refresh

## Why It Matters

People decide whether to click on your music in 0.5 seconds based on the artwork. A consistent visual identity means fans recognize your posts without reading your name. The biggest artists in the world have visual brands as strong as their sonic ones.

- **Tyler, The Creator:** Every album era has a distinct, obsessive visual world
- **The Weeknd:** "After Hours" red suit became iconic before anyone heard the album
- **Billie Eilish:** The slime green/black era was a visual brand that transcended music
- **Frank Ocean:** Minimal, intimate, analog photography — matches the music perfectly

## Brand Elements Checklist

### 1. Color Palette

Every artist needs 3-5 colors:

```
PRIMARY:    [hex] — Your main color. Used most often.
SECONDARY:  [hex] — Complement to primary.
ACCENT:     [hex] — For highlights, CTAs, emphasis.
DARK:       [hex] — Backgrounds, contrast.
LIGHT:      [hex] — Text on dark backgrounds.
```

**How to choose:**
- Match the mood of your music (dark R&B ≠ bright pop colors)
- Pull from your best press photo or a reference image you love
- Use coolors.co to generate palettes
- Test on a mock cover art — does it feel like your sound?

### 2. Typography

Pick 2 fonts maximum:

```
DISPLAY FONT:  [Name] — For headlines, logos, cover art titles
BODY FONT:     [Name] — For captions, bios, body text
```

**Free font sources:** Google Fonts, Font Squirrel, DaFont
**Paid (better):** Type Network, Klim Type Foundry, Future Fonts

### 3. Logo / Wordmark

Options:
- **Wordmark:** Your artist name in a distinctive font (most common, most versatile)
- **Logo mark:** A symbol/icon (harder to establish, but powerful once recognized)
- **Combination:** Both (best of both worlds)

**DIY tools:** Canva (free), Figma (free), Adobe Express
**Professional:** $200-2,000 from a designer (worth it if you can afford it)

**Rules:**
- Must work in black AND white
- Must be legible at small sizes (phone screen)
- Must work on a square format (album art, profile pic)
- Don't use more than 2 fonts in the logo

### 4. Press Photos

Minimum 3 photos in rotation:

```
LANDSCAPE:   Wide shot for banners, headers, press
PORTRAIT:    Vertical for Spotify profile, Instagram feed
PERFORMANCE: Live shot for booking materials
```

**Specifications:**
- Resolution: 3000px minimum on longest side
- Color space: sRGB for digital, CMYK for print
- Format: JPEG and TIFF/PNG
- Photographer credit always included
- Get a photo release / usage rights in writing

**Budget options:**
- Photography student ($50-150 per session)
- Trade with a photographer (they get content for portfolio)
- iPhone with good lighting (golden hour, indirect window light)

**What makes a good press photo:**
- Clean background (not your messy bedroom)
- Good lighting (natural > flash)
- Genuine expression (not a stock photo smile)
- Consistent with your brand mood
- NO selfies for press materials

### 5. Cover Art Direction

For consistency across releases:

```
COVER ART GUIDELINES

Layout:        [centered text / off-center / no text / etc.]
Font:          [Display font from brand guide]
Color palette: [Use brand colors]
Photo style:   [Film grain / crisp digital / illustrated / abstract]
Recurring elements: [Any motifs — a specific symbol, border style, etc.]
Dimensions:    3000x3000px minimum (required by all distributors)
Format:        JPEG, RGB, <20MB
```

**Cover art tiers:**
- **DIY (free):** Canva, Figma templates, iPhone photo + free editing
- **Stock + design ($20-50):** Unsplash/Pexels photo + Canva Pro design
- **Freelance designer ($100-500):** Fiverr, 99designs, local designer
- **Professional ($500-2,000+):** Full creative direction, photo shoot, design

### 6. Social Media Templates

Create reusable templates for:
- **Announcement posts** (new release, show, merch drop)
- **Quote/lyric cards** (text over branded background)
- **Behind-the-scenes** (consistent filter/edit style)
- **Story templates** (countdown, Q&A, poll frames)

Tools: Canva (free templates), Figma, Adobe Express

## Brand Guidelines Document

Create a one-page brand guide:

```
[ARTIST NAME] — BRAND GUIDELINES

COLORS:
  Primary:   #_____  [swatch]
  Secondary: #_____  [swatch]
  Accent:    #_____  [swatch]
  Dark:      #_____  [swatch]
  Light:     #_____  [swatch]

TYPOGRAPHY:
  Display:   [Font Name] — headings, logos, titles
  Body:      [Font Name] — captions, bios

LOGO:
  [Logo placement — files stored at ____]
  Min size: 100px wide
  Clear space: logo height on all sides
  Never stretch, rotate, or recolor

PHOTOGRAPHY:
  Style: [describe — moody, bright, film, etc.]
  Photographer: [name + contact]
  Files: [location]

TONE:
  [3-5 words that describe your visual vibe]
  Examples: "dark, intimate, analog" or "bright, bold, street"

DO:
  ✓ Use brand colors consistently
  ✓ Maintain the same photo editing style
  ✓ Use the designated fonts

DON'T:
  ✗ Use different fonts on every post
  ✗ Use random colors not in the palette
  ✗ Use low-resolution images
  ✗ Put your entire bio on the cover art
```

## Era / Project Branding

For albums or EP campaigns, create a visual era:

```
PROJECT: "[Album/EP Title]"

ERA COLORS:    [Modified palette for this project]
ERA MOOD:      [Visual keywords — "neon noir," "desert warmth," etc.]
COVER ART:     [Description of direction]
PHOTO STYLE:   [How press photos differ from core brand]
SOCIAL THEME:  [Visual content approach for this campaign]
MERCH DESIGN:  [How merch ties into the era visually]

This era runs from [start date] to [end date or "next project"].
After this era, return to core brand guidelines.
```
